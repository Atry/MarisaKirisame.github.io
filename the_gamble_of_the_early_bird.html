The gamble of the early bird

Let's say you haven't graduate from high school yet (Or else you don't need to read this).
Let's also say that you have an idea of your future profession (for example, you want to be a programmer).
What should you do to make your dream come true?
Obviously, there's not anything you can do, beside studying/practicing your future profession now. But should you?
Like everyone else, you have 24 hour per day, and if you want to spent time on it, you have to draw you time on something else – school.
Is it worth doing so? It depend! But no matter what you choose, this is a life-changing gamble.
And this article is to help you analyze, and to make your decision.

First, let's look at your chip – what you will sacrifice.

1 : Academic Performance: this is obvious.

2 : General Education: School does not just randomly choose some topic and stuff it in the agenda: it will probably have some use later on.

3 : Your future University Ranking.
Your standardize test score will drop in accordance to your Academic Performance.
And no, unless you are THAT good(something in the level of winning international competition), your loss cannot be compensated by what you gain – remember, the one reading your personal statement don't have the time to distinguish you from someone who fake their extracurricular activities using Wikipedia.

What will you gain?

1 : Decisive amount of Knowledge and Experience in your field.
If you start specializing in junior high, you'll have spent 6-7 years on your subject – the time it took to go from undergrad to get a master degree.
While due to no-guidance, you probably will be less effective to learn by yourself, it is enough to enable you to stay top-notch against your peer.
Even if you start later/put in less effort, it is significantly better than having virtually none of it.

2 : Habit/Knowledge that you acquire along.
You probably will develop a good study/work habit, have better self-discipline, and know how to do academic research on your own.
You will also learn about the prerequisite knowledge(for example, discrete mathematic if you are doing CS) of your field.

3 : University that suit you better.
If you have not decide your major, how will you choose a university that can give you top-notch education?
Ooh, let's apply to havard/mit*!
What if you decided to be a programmer?
That's easy, apply to the big four(CMU, MIT, UCB, Stanford*)!
What if you decided to do Formal Verification(Don't worry if you don't know what it is, you just need to know it's a branch of CS)?
CMU, MIT, Texas Austin, UW*!
As you can see, as you focus on more specific field, the competition will be easier, and you can receive better education in the field.

What else should you consider before deciding?

1 : How much prerequisite are there? If the answer is “a lot”, it help to do good in school.

2 : Does it need formal teaching/special equipment? Think twice if you want to learn chemistry on your own.

3 : How much of your skill can transfer after you graduate from academia? It doesn't make sense to learn Symbian app development now.

4 : What will you do in university? You should come up of a better answer than “relearning everything”.

5 : How much work does it take to grasp the basic knowledge in your field?
It doesn't make sense to learn “half of a language”:
you need to put all the piece together so that it make sense to you intuitively, or you will forget it, sooner or later.
If the key concept is difficult to learn, think twice if you do not want to spend too much time specializing.

If you had decided that you will specialize early, here's what you also need to do.

1 : Talk to your parent. Even in the worse case, they are your income source, and you cant go far without their support.

2 : Join some on-line community. It will help you to learn, and connect you to future career opportunity/really cool and helpful people.

3 : Do some research on how the field actually is.
If you do not do so, your depiction of what you will do may be hopelessly wrong.
Don't let your inaccurate perception have a chance to determine the rest of your life.

That's it. If you have question don't hesitate to email me/others, since your decision will have great impact on yourself.
